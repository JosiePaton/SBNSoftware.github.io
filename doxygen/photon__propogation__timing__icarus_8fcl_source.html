2 # File: photon_propagation_timing_icarus.fcl
3 # Purpose: parameters of the time of arrival to PMT of scintillation light
4 # Date: 20201002
5 # Author: Patrick Green (data),
6 # Gianluca Petrillo (petrillo@slac.stanford.edu) (packaging)
7 #
8 # The time of arrivals of scintillation photons to PMT is parametrized with
9 # a Landau or (Landau plus Exponential) functional form, as function of the
10 # displacement between the scintillation point and the PMT.
11 # In particular, both the modulus of the displacement (distance) and the
12 # incident angle on the PMT are relevant.
13 # The parametrization currently covers only the hemispace the PMT face,
14 # while light from behind (which must incur major scattering in order to hit
15 # the sensitive surface of the hemispherical PMT) is not included.
16 #
17 # ICARUS does not have reflective foils or wavelength shifters inside
18 # the detector volume (apart from the coating on the PMT), so all the light is
19 # at wavelength aroudn 128 nm (VUV): the visible/reflected components are not
20 # present and not needed.
21 #
22 # The content of this file includes:
23 #
24 # * photon_propagation_timing_icarus: the parametrization of choice of ICARUS;
25 # defined as alias of another parametrization
26 # * photon_propagation_timing_icarus_protodune: settings for ProtoDUNE,
27 # imported from opticalsimparameterisations_dune.fcl of dunetpc repository
28 # tag v09_05_00; these values were plugged in there by Patrick Green
29 # (SBND, DUNE) on September 23, 2020.
30 # * photon_propagation_timing_icarus_v09_05_00: settings for SBND, modified
31 # to correctly extrapolate to ICARUS distances; these values were the
32 # default settings in LArSoft v09_05_00, and they have been recommended
33 # for ICARUS by Patrick Green and Diego Garcia Gamez (SBND), with the
34 # belief that ICARUS-derived parameetrs will not relevantly differ.
35 #
